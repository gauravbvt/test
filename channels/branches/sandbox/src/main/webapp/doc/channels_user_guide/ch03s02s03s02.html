Effect-driven strategy

Figure 3.12. Effect-driven IS plan building strategy


Planners start with the onset of an event phase, add tasks that are either directly started by it or are ongoing at the onset. They then add the information flows from these tasks to other tasks that are triggered or terminated by the shared information. Planners indicate whether tasks initiate or terminate events. This process continues until no task gets triggered or terminated by information produced by specified tasks, and all event phases are covered.

Systematic use of an effect-driven strategy is akin to telling a story from beginning to end, and thus may feel more natural to many planners. However some tasks and flows added using this strategy might not necessarily contribute directly or indirectly to achieving identified goals. In other words, a "starting from the beginning" strategy does not guarantee that it will reach all required end points.
