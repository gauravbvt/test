Information sharing agreements

An information sharing agreement is a formal recognition that an organization commits to share certain information with another organization.

Channels finds the information sharing commitments of agents between each pair of organizations and, for each "networking" pair, aggregates the commitments into distinct "information sharing relationships". Each information sharing relationship is a summary of sharing commitments for particular information (the agents in the commitments and their roles are abstracted out). For example, an information sharing relationship would summarize the fact that members of Bank A commit to share fraud information with members of Bank B. For each such relationship, planners can indicate whether an information sharing commitment has been formalized, and attach or link to the document formalizing the agreement.

Figure 1.36. The sharing agreements of an organization


An organization may require that its sharing commitments with other organizations be covered by sharing agreements. If there is an exception to this rule, an issue is raised by Channels. Furthermore, if a sharing agreement is confirmed but not documented, an issue is also raised. Note that in applying these constraints, Channels does not distinguish when both organizations belong to a common parent organization and when they do not.
