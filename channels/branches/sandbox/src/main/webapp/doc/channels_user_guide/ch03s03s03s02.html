Profiling a phase

A phase represents time relative to events. A phase can either represent the time before, during, or after events. Planners decide which phases to include within the scope of an IS plan. By default, a new IS plan has a "Responding" phase that deals with information sharing during events.

A phase is always a type.

Table 3.13. Profiling: Profile a phase
Prerequisites:

  • The phase was named
Steps:

(All steps are optional and they can be taken in any order)

  • Change the name of the phase

  • Describe the phase

  • Tag the phase

  • Attach a document (policy or reference)

  • Characterize the phase as either pre-event, co-event, or post-event
Outcomes:

  • Depending on its categorization, the event may or may not be compatible as a category for certain other events.

  • Changing the nature of a phase (pre- vs. co- vs. post-event) changes what it means for tasks to be started with or to terminate this phase of an event.
