Profiling a transmission medium

A transmission medium is a means by which information can be shared. The means may involve technology (phone, instant messaging, email, etc.) or it may not (face-to-face). A communication channel is a transmission medium combined with an endpoint (aka address). For example, a communication channel might be "Phone" (transmission medium) with "1-800-234-5678" (endpoint), or "email" with "jane.doe@example.com".

A transmission medium is always a type.

Table 3.18. Profiling: Profile a transmission medium
Prerequisites:

  • The transmission medium was named
Steps:

(All steps are optional and they can be taken in any order)

  • Change the name of the transmission medium

  • Describe the transmission medium

  • Tag the transmission medium

  • Categorize the transmission medium with other transmission media (avoiding circular categorization)

  • Attach picture, policy, or reference document

  • Specify the mode of transmission as either unicast (single, known recipient), multicast (many, known recipients), or broadcast (many, unknown recipients)

  • List the transmission media this medium delegates to; for example, a notification system would delegate to phone, email, pager, etc.

  • Give the pattern for valid addresses/endpoints for this medium - a regular expression (see http://www.regular-expressions.info/)

  • Give the list of secrecy classifications of information this medium is considered a secure carrier for
Outcomes:

  • Depending on its categorization, the transmission medium may or may not be compatible as a category for certain other transmission media.

  • The applicability of a transmission medium may depend on its nature (unicast vs. multicast vs. broadcast) and its security classification. Changing the acceptable pattern for endpoints may render some channels invalid.
