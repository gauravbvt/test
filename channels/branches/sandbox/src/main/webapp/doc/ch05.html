Lexicon

Glossary

Agent

An agent is a person or system who can be assigned tasks in an information Sharing Plan based on its profile.

Archetype

A stand in for agents that need not be individually named and profiled. An example would be “Call center employee”. An agent archetype can play multiple roles and have contact info.

Broadcast transmission

A transmission that could be received by any number of unidentified agents.

Channel

A communication channel specifies a means for the transmission of information. A channel is defined by a transmission medium and, where appropriate, an endpoint.

Channel endpoint

A channel endpoint specifies who can receive the transmission of information in a given medium. An email address, radio channel, or phone number are examples of endpoints.

Element of information

An element of information is a named component of information. An element of information may be classified and can be accompanied with handling and sourcing data.

Entity

An entity in ISP is something that can be referenced by name in the definition of one or more segments, tasks, flows or entities. Entities are agents, transmission media, events, organizations, phases, places or roles.

Event

An event in ISP is an anticipated change in the environment of organizations that puts their mission at risk.

Gain

A gain is a potential positive outcome for an organization caused by an event.

Goal

A goal is the intent by an organization to mitigate a risk to itself or to realize a gain during a phase of an event.

Immediate task

An immediate task starts with an event phase.

Incident

An incident is an “outside” event, that is, an event not expected to be caused by a task specified in the Plan.

Information

Actionable data.

Information sharing commitment

An information sharing commitment exists between all agents assigned to task producing information and all agents assigned to the task consuming it.

Information sharing flow

An information sharing specifies a commitment to share information produced from the execution of a task for use in the execution of another task.

Information sharing plan

An Information Sharing Plan specifies the Procedures that people and systems in roles of responsibility are expected to apply in order to fulfill the missions of their organizations before, during and/or after certain types of events.

Information sharing procedure

An information sharing procedure for an agent is either a task assigned to the agent or a sharing commitment by the agent.

Issue

An issue is a problem with an element of an information sharing plan or with the plan as a whole. Issues can be about validity (incorrect definition), completeness (something missing) or robustness (plan is failure-prone).

Jurisdiction

The location or geographical area for which a role is assigned to an agent by an organization.

Multicast transmission

A transmission targeting multiple, identified agents.

Organization

An organization is a group of persons and systems assigned roles of responsibility and organized to accomplish a mission.

Participant

A participant in an Information Sharing Plan is a person or system who is assigned tasks.

Permanent task

A permanent task is expected to be ongoing for the duration of an event phase.

Phase

A phase is time relative to the initiation or termination of an event. A pre-event phase is time before the initiation of the event. A co-event phase is time between initiation and termination of an event, and a post-event phase is time after termination of an event.

Planner

A planner is a person authorized to modify an Information Sharing Plan.

Policy

A policy is an authoritative statement by an organization as to what is allowed, mandated or forbidden.

Role

A role names implied responsibilities assigned by organizations to agents.

Risk

A risk is a potential negative outcome for an organization caused by an event.

Scope

The scope of an Information Sharing Plan is comprised of the events and event phases that are planned for, the organizational goals to be met, the participating organizations and their participating members.

Segment

A segment is a manageable subset of an Information Sharing Plan that should include only the tasks and flows needed to achieve one or more stated goals for one phase of one event.

Task

A task is a purposeful action to be carried out separately or as a team by agents.

Task assignment

A task is assigned to an agent if the agent matches the specifications of who is to execute the task.

Transmission medium

A transmission medium is a technological or non-technological means for transmitting information. A transmission medium can support unicast, multicast or broadcast transmissions.

Unicast transmission

A transmission targeting a single, identified agent.
