Benefits and deliverables

Information Sharing Planning captures, analyzes and deploys knowledge of how organizations need to share information to mitigate systemic risks and seize opportunities from anticipated events.

Information Sharing Planning (ISP) produces information sharing procedures for event preparedness, response and recovery. Information Sharing Plans capture commitments to share the information required for organizations to fulfill their missions.

ISP uncovers:

  • who needs what information, and why and when,

  • who has or can produce needed information, and how and when,

  • what information should flow, and when and how,

  • the consequences of not sharing according to Plan.

The deliverables of ISP are:

  • documented information sharing procedures

  • information flow analytics and visualizations,

  • detected information sharing gaps, bottlenecks and single points of failure.

It is important understand that an Information Sharing Plan is not an action plan. Its objective is not to dictate a sequence of actions.[1]Information sharing planning concerns itself exclusively with capturing information sharing requirements for necessary tasks to be carried out successfully.


[1] However, task sequencing is not entirely absent from ISP. A task may require information produced by another task that would then necessarily precede it. These sequencing constraints do not typically apply to all tasks and they certainly do not dictate a unique ordering for all tasks. In any case, attempting to define an action plan for organizations that don't have a central authority is more often than not an exercise in futility. What really matters here is capturing what needs to be done, what information is required and produced, and how and when it should or should not be shared.
