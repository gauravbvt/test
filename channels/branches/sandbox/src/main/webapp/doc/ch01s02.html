Benefits and deliverables

Information Sharing Planning captures, analyzes and deploys knowledge of how organizations need to share information to mitigate systemic risks and seize opportunities from anticipated events.

Information Sharing Planning (ISP) produces information sharing procedures for event preparedness, response and recovery. Information Sharing Plans capture commitments to share the information required for organizations to fulfill their missions.

ISP uncovers:

  • who needs what information, and why and when,

  • who has or can produce needed information, and how and when,

  • what information should flow, and when and how,

  • the consequences of not sharing according to Plan,

  • where shared information originally comes from and ultimately ends up.

The deliverables of ISP are:

  • documented information sharing procedures

  • information flow analytics and visualizations,

  • detected information sharing gaps, bottlenecks and single points of failure.

It is important understand that an Information Sharing Plan is not an action plan; it's objective is not to dictate a sequence of actions nor is it to optimize processes.[1]Information sharing planning concerns itself exclusively with capturing the information sharing requirements of tasks that must be executed to manage disruptive events.


[1] However, task sequencing is not entirely absent from ISP. The execution of a task may require information produced by the prior execution of another task. These sequencing constraints do not typically apply to all tasks and they certainly do not dictate a unique ordering for all tasks. The fact that an IS Plan does not dictate a strict sequence of tasks is a good thing. Attempting to define an action plan for organizations that don't have a central authority can be an exercise in futility. What really matters here is capturing what needs to be done and then how information must flow to make it possible.
