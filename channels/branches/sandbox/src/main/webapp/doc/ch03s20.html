Administration

A Channels administrator (and only an administrator) can create new plans, retire existing plans, register and disable users, and assign plan-access privileges to them. Administrators are also responsible for configuring an installation of Channels.

Plan creation and user privileges

The Administration page is accessible only to the administrator of Channels. The Administration Page enables an administration to:

  • Select a Plan as the current Plan

  • Access the Plan's Procedures (from the development version)

  • Access the Plan editor (by clicking on "Modify Plan")

  • Create a new Plan by:

    • Giving it a unique and fixed URI

    • Identifying the sponsor (client)

    • Giving the support community's ID (more on that later)

  • Put the development version of the current Plan into production (by clicking on "Productize Plan")

  • Retire the current Plan

  • Add a user (with user id, email address and password)

  • Assign user privileges (Planner or User) for the current Plan or for all Plans

  • Disable a user

  • Give another user Administrator privileges

  • Identify the support community ID for a Plan

External services

Channels relies on a number of online services:

  • Geonames and Google MapsTM for geolocation

  • SurveyGizmosTM for launching surveys and collecting the results

  • An email service (SMTP) to send emails

  • BetaEasyTM for providing feedback and for online community support

Access to an external service requires account identification, either in the form of user id and password or in the form of an API key. When Channels is used as a service, service configuration is already taken care of. However when installing a new instance of Channels, an Administrator must provide purchase service access and configure Channels to use the correct account information. [2]

Planners can log into the BetaEasyTM feedback service and the SurveyGizmoTM survey service to browse collected data and get reports.

Feedback service: BetaEasyTM

Before creating a new Plan, the Administrator normally creates two distinct support communities, one for the planners and one for the users of the to-be-created Plan. The administrator creates BetaEasy accounts for all planners and sends out login information to them.

Each support community is implemented as a BetaEasy project. The community is identified by a BetaEasy project ID. A default support community is pre-defined for all Plans that don't identity their own support communities.

It is the responsibility of the administrator to give each planner access to the BetaEasy projects that correspond to the Plans they are assigned to. After logging in, the planner can see reports on the feedback of other planners and of users. Users provide their feedback about their Procedures via the feedback widget from within the Information Sharing Procedures report. Planners provide their feedback about the Plan from within the Plan editor.

To browse and mine the feedback of other planners about a Plan or of users about their Procedures, a planner must first log into the BetaEasy service.

After logging in, a BetaEasyTM manager (and ISP planner), is shown a dashboard summarizing the feedback activity on all authorized projects.

Planners can then browse the collected feedback.

Refer to BetaEasyTM's online help at http://www.betaeasy.com/help for more details on how to use their service.

Survey service: SurveyGizmoTM

Surveys launched and managed from within Channels are hosted and managed by the SurveyGizmoTM service.

A planner can access a survey sample or a survey report directly from within Channels.

A planner can look at the questionnaire for a particular survey and see a report on the response to that survey.

Refer to the SurveyGizmoTM service's online help at http://surveygizmo.zendesk.com/home for more details on how to use their service.


[2] The installation and configuration of Channels is covered in the "Channels Installation and Configuration Guide".
