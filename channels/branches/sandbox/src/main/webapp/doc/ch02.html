Information sharing planners (Planners) apply the ISP Method to build information sharing plans (Plans) from which robust information sharing procedures (Procedures) can be generated. These Procedures enable participating organizations to fulfill their missions before, during and/or after one or more events of significance.

The ISP Method defines:

  • the conceptual building blocks of a Plan,

  • the activities by which a Plan is built, with prerequisites, steps and outcomes,

  • strategies for moving a Plan to completion,

  • and issues that may keep a Plan from being valid, complete and robust

ISP Method and ISP Process

The ISP Method is part of the ISP Process. The ISP Process adds to the ISP Method Best Practices for the successful promotion, definition, initiation and execution of information sharing planning projects.

ISP Method activities (Activities) should not be confused with Best Practices. For example, a Best Practice might help planners decide what the scope a Plan should be, whereas an Activity would prescribe how to capture the scope of a Plan (once decided upon) and indicate which Activities are triggered by changes to the scope.
