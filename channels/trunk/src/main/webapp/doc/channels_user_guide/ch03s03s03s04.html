Profiling an organization

Organizations assign their member agents to roles of responsibility, possibly under supervision and for some jurisdiction. Member agents of an organization are expected to carry out tasks to mitigate risks or make gains before, during, or after events of significance. Failure to do so imperils the organization's mission.

Organization entities can be either actual organizations or types of organizations.

Table 3.15. Profiling: Profile an organization
Prerequisites:

  • The organization or type of organization was named.
Steps:

(All steps are optional and they can be taken in any order)

  • Change the name of the organization

  • Describe the organization

  • Tag the organization

  • Categorize the organization with other organizations (avoiding circular categorization)

  • Attach a document (picture, policy, or reference)

  • The organization's mission - as unstructured text

For actual organizations:

  • The name of the actual parent organization

  • The name of the place where the organization is officially located

  • Contact information - a list of communication channels (channel = transmission medium + "endpoint")

  • Whether cross-organizational information sharing commitments are allowed without sharing agreements

  • Whether all roles known to be assignable by the organization must be assigned to at least one agent

  • A list of jobs, where each job is defined as:

    • the name of an agent

    • the agent's title (for example, Senior VP)

    • the agent's role

    • the agent's jurisdiction in that role

    • the agent's supervisor (an agent name)

    • whether the agent is confirmed in this job (as opposed to implicitly in this job from the definition of a task)

  • The confirmed sharing agreements that formalize cross-organizational information sharing commitments by the organization, with attached Memoranda of Understanding

For types of organization:

  • The name of the parent organization (actual or type)
Outcomes:

  • Depending on its categorization, a type of organization may or may not be compatible as a category for certain other organizations.

  • Assignments, and thus sharing commitments, may change for tasks that specify an organization (most typically do).

The title of an agent as member of an organization is used only in the generation of contact lists in the IS guidelines.

If the same agent plays more than one role in an organization, then the agent has more than one job.

It is recommended to always identify the supervisor of an agent in each of the roles assigned.
