How is ISP different from BPM?

The most frequently asked question about Information Sharing Planning is "How is different from Business Process Management"?

To answer this question, one must first consider that Information Sharing Planning (ISP) is part of something larger called Information Sharing Management.

Information Sharing Management (ISM) is a set of technologies and best practices that help communities of organizations share information more effectively in order to achieve their missions when preparing for, responding to, and recovering from actual events. Information Sharing Planning (ISP) produces the information sharing procedures that organizations apply when actually sharing information. ISP analyzes these procedures for continuous improvement.

Business Process Management (BPM) helps enterprises become more efficient, more effective, and more agile in how they each operate. Business Process Modeling captures and analyzes business processes for continuous improvement.

Because ISP relates most closely to Business Process Modeling, the question should be recast to "How do Information Sharing Management and Business Process Management differ"?

Other questions are "Can ISM and BPM be used together"? and "How do the markets for BPM and ISM compare"?

Differences

BPM and ISM tackle different problems from different perspectives.

BPM focuses "inward" on a single (but possibly extended) enterprise. It looks at how the enterprise responds internally to events, and at how it renders services to its customers. The end is maximizing value creation while minimizing costs. The means is business process improvement.

ISM focuses "outward" on (often ad hoc) communities of organizations. It looks at how organizations depend on one another to get the information they need to execute tasks that achieve organizational goals. The end is minimizing the impacts of systemic risks and maximizing the capture of opportunities. The means is better information sharing.

While there is conceptual overlap (both ISM and BPM involve tasks, events, and communications) and even though they both use somewhat similar-looking visualizations (directed graphs), ISM and BPM have very different world views. BPM looks at the operations of an enterprise, whereas ISM looks at the information flows between a community of organizations. They also differ in their goals: BPM optimizes enterprise value creation whereas ISM reduces the impacts of systemic risks.
