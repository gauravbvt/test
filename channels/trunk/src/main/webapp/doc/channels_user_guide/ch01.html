Why plan the sharing of information?

Most organizations now operate in complex "ecosystems" where they depend on each other for information critical to their missions. It is common wisdom that getting the right information at the right time is essential to achieving mission success. This is even more so in a connected world where opportunities can be fleeting and risks can spread not only quickly but far and wide.

Yet most organizations do not plan how they should share information within and across organizational boundaries. It is something of a paradox that while they will plan their finances or model their business processes to achieve greater efficiency, they will rarely define how they need to communicate to better prevent, respond or recover from crises.

Failure to plan how information should flow can cost lives, money, reputation. During a crisis, information will not spontaneously flow as needed and maintaining contact lists is clearly not enough. Organizations must prepare and create actionable information sharing procedures. Information sharing can and should be planned not only to be prepared but also to capture, retain and disseminate communication best practices.

Information Sharing Planning (ISP) models, analyzes and deploys knowledge of how organizations need to share information to mitigate systemic risks and seize opportunities from anticipated events.

ISP provides a framework and method for designing and improving the information sharing procedures required by networked organizations to achieve their missions.
