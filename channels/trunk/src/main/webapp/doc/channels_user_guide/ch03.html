Channels enables information sharing planners to apply the ISP Method, an incremental and nonlinear process for building information sharing plans from which useful analytics and robust information sharing procedures can be generated.

IS analytics enable planners to better understand the consequences and weaknesses of a plan while IS procedures enable participating organizations to more reliably fulfill their missions through information sharing, before, during, and/or after one or more events of significance.

The ISP Method defines:

  • The conceptual building blocks of a plan (see previous chapter)

  • The activities by which a plan is built, with prerequisites, steps, and outcomes

  • Strategies for moving a plan to completion

  • Issues that may keep a plan from being deemed valid, complete, and robust

ISP Method and ISP Process

The ISP Method is encompassed by the ISP Process. The ISP Process includes the ISP Method and adds best practices for the successful promotion, definition, initiation, implementation, and review of information sharing planning projects. The ISP Process, other than its significance to the ISP Method, is outside the scope of this document.

Figure 3.2. Where the ISP Method fits within the ISP Process


The ISP Method is defined in terms of the ISP concepts covered earlier and of ISP Method activities (activities for short). These activities should not be confused with ISP Process best practices. For example, a ISP best practice might help planners decide what the scope an IS plan should be, whereas a plan scoping activity would indicate any prerequisite activities, prescribe how to capture the scope of a plan (once decided upon), and indicate what further activities could follow up changes to the scope.
