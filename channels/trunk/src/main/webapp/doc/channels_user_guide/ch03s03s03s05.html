Profile a role

The assignees to tasks are typically specified in terms of their roles and the organizations or types of organizations they belong to. Roles should not be specific to organizations but cut across organizations. It would be counter-productive to have a Chief of Security at one organization and a Head of Security at another when both roles have identical responsibilities.

How types are categorized by other types, that is, the typology of roles in an IS plan, is a key variable in the computation of task assignments and thus information sharing commitments.

Table 3.16. Profiling: Profile a role
Prerequisites:

  • The role was named
Steps:

(All steps are optional and they can be taken in any order)

  • Change the name of the role

  • Describe the role

  • Tag the role

  • Categorize the role with other roles (avoiding circular categorization)

  • Attach picture, policy or reference document
Outcomes:

  • Depending on its categorization, the role may or may not be compatible as a category for certain other roles.
