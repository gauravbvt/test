Information flow map

The information flow map displayed in the Plan Editor provides a visualization of how information ought to flow in the situation that is the focus of the currently selected segment.

Figure 2.25. Maximized flow map for a segment


Tasks are the nodes (icons over text) in the graph. Information needs, capabilities, and sharing flows are represented by the arrows. Tasks and flows are drawn in red if they have known issues. Green and red traffic lights represent the start and end of the event phase covered by the segment. Tasks that are triggered by the start of the segment are connected to the green traffic light icon. Tasks that terminate the event phase are linked to the red traffic light.

Information flows (information shared), information needs (information needed for carrying out, triggering, or terminating a task), and information capabilities (shareable information produced by carrying out a task) are shown as arrows. Single-headed arrows represent notifications. Double-headed arrows are request-replies, with the empty arrowhead pointing to the task that consumes the information request, and the solid arrowhead pointing to the task that consumes the answer (the requested information flows to it).

Small squares stand for to-be-determined tasks. An arrow from a task to a dot represents an information capability of the task (information produced by the task that can be shared for use in other, to-be-determined tasks). An arrow from a small square to a task represents an information need of the task (the task needs information possibly produced by other, to-be-determined tasks).

When risks and goals are shown in a flow map, they appear respectively as colored flags and stars connected to tasks (if they achieve a goal) and segment end icons (if goals are achieved by ending the segment's event phase).

Tasks within a blue-gray rectangle denote tasks from other segments that are connected by sharing flows to tasks in the segment being viewed.

The flow map legend is shown by clicking on the "Help" icon in the flow map's display controls.

Figure 2.26. Flow map legend


Flow map display controls

The flow map display controls at the top left corner of a flow map give planners options as to how the flow map is displayed. The controls allow planners to:

  • Show/hide information needs and capabilities

  • Show/hide goals achieved by tasks

  • Show/hide tasks and flows not marked as operational

  • Maximize/un-maximize the flow map

  • Reduce the flow map to fit

  • Show the flow map legend

Figure 2.27. Flow map view controls


Flow maps can become quite large. A flow map can be reduced to fit within the available space by clicking on the "resize-to-fit" view control. Clicking on the icon once more brings the flow map back to its full size.

Figure 2.28. Flow map displayed at full size


Figure 2.29. Flow map resized to fit


A flow map can be maximized to use all of the screen space allocated to the Plan Editor by clicking on the "maximize" view control. A maximized flow map can also be reduced to fit. A flow map is un-maximized either by clicking once more on the "maximize" view control or by selecting a task or a flow within the map.

Figure 2.30. Maximized flow map


Figure 2.31. Maximized flow map resized to fit


A flow map, by default, does not display risks and gains (goals) and how they relate to tasks or the segment as a whole. To display risks and gains, the planner must click on the "show goals" control. Clicking again hides the goals.

Figure 2.32. Flow map showing goals achieved by tasks


A flow map, also by default, does not display information needs and capabilities (tasks connected by arrows to square dots). To display information needs and capabilities the planner must click on the "show needs and capabilities" control. Clicking again hides them.

Figure 2.33. Flow map showing information needs and capabilities


Tasks and flow are by default marked as "operational." Planners can change the operational status of tasks and flows and then hide nonoperational tasks and flows from the flow map.

Figure 2.34. Flow map showing nonoperational tasks and flows


Figure 2.35. Flow map hiding nonoperational tasks and flows


The planner can expand the height of the task, receives, and sends panels at the bottom, at the expense of the flow map, by pressing the "expand forms" view control. Clicking again un-expands the bottom panels.
