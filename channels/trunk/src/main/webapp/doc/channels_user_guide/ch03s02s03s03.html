Issue-driven strategy

Figure 3.16. Issue-driven IS plan building strategy


When using an issue-driven strategy, planners scan the list of outstanding issues and determine which ones to either waive or resolve. Issues of validity ought to be dealt with first, followed by issues of completeness and then issues of robustness. Usually, everything else being equal, issues of higher severity should be given higher priority for remediation.

A planner selects a high-priority issue and either waives it (if applicable) or applies one of the recommended remediations, possibly after having queried other planners and/or participants. The process is repeated until no unwaived issue remains.

A plan with no outstanding, “unwaived” issues means that Channels and the planners considers the plan to be valid, complete and robust. Because Channels has no domain knowledge (for example, it knows nothing about public health or mergers and acquisitions etc.), domain-dependent issues will not be detected by Channels. Planners must use their domain knowledge to determine if the IS plan has issues that lie outside the range of automatically detected issues.
