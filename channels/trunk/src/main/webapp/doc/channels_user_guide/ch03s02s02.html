Issues

The ISP Method defines a relatively large number (90+) of issues that can be detected in an IS plan. They are categorized as:

  • Validity issues - something is incorrectly specified or defined

  • Completeness issues - something is apparently missing

  • Robustness issues - something makes the plan inherently failure-prone

Figure 3.3. Summary of issues found in an IS plan


An issue can be about the IS plan as a whole, or about a segment, a task, a flow, an entity, or any other kind of plan element.

An issue can be assigned a severity of minor, major, severe, or extreme. The severity of an issue is either preset according to the kind of issue (for example, an information flow without elements of information is always a major issue), or computed by determining the most severe risk mitigation or gain capture that could be foiled by a cascade of failures caused by the issue.

An issue comes with one or more alternate options for its remediation. A planner can elect to launch a survey to gather opinions on how to remediate an issue.

Figure 3.4. Issue detected on a task


All types of issues defined in the ISP Method can be detected by Channels. Some issue detections are “best guesses” and can be waived by planners if they are deemed to be false positives.

Figure 3.5. Issues detected on a flow that can be waived


Planners can report ad hoc issues that are not in the list of 90+ predefined kinds of ISP issues Issues reported by planners will typically be domain-specific (all of the ISP issue types are domain-agnostic).
